El
Filibusterismo

"El Filibusterismo" is a novel written by José Rizal, published in 1891. It serves as a sequel to his earlier work, "Noli Me Tangere," and continues the narrative of the protagonist, Crisostomo Ibarra, who returns as the mysterious jeweler Simoun. Set in the late 19th century during the Spanish colonial period in the Philippines, the novel revolves around themes of social injustice, oppression, and resistance.

El
Filibusterismo

"El Filibusterismo" is deeply entrenched in the colonial era of the Ph under Spanish rule. Through his writing, Rizal aimed to ignite awareness and solidarity among his compatriots, urging them towards freedom and equality. Today, "El Filibusterismo" stands as a cornerstone of Ph literature, celebrated for its poignant depiction of colonial society addressing themes of social justice and national identity.

El Filibusterismo

The novel centers on the Noli-El fili duology's main character Crisóstomo Ibarra, now returning for vengeance as "Simoun". The novel's dark theme departs dramatically from the previous novel's hopeful and romantic atmosphere, signifying Ibarra's resort to solving his country's issues through violent means, after his previous attempt in reforming the country's system made no effect and seemed impossible with the corrupt attitude of the Spaniards toward the Filipinos.
